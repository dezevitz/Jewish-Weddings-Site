Hello!

This website was made by me, Danielle Zevitz, for my Jewish Weddings class. Since I come from an interfaith family, I wanted to study interfaith weddings! If you go to the biography section of the website (under the about tab), you can hear more about the bride and groom and how they met. Under the schedule section, you can see the menu and the schedule. This is where the bulk of my research shows. Muslims and Jews both have dietary restrictions that are similar, and thats why I had my characters meet in the kosher food section of the grocery store. Many Muslims will actually eat Kosher food, because the Halal and Kosher dietary restrictions are very similar and Halal meats are harder to find in America. The schedule takes the most important Jewish and Muslim traditions, and combines the Muslim and Jewish wedding contract because they are very similar. Under the portfolio section, you can see the wedding contract that I created. I tried to use terminology from both religions and included translations and dates from both religions. You can click on the images to zoom in and hover over the images to read their description. Finally, on the RSVP page you continue to learn more about the family relationship but in more of a subtle way. Miriam's family is orthodox and not supportive of the wedding, so not only are they not coming to the wedding, but they also refuse to comment or acknowledge the website. Mohammed's family is much more supportive. Hope you enjoy my webpage! Here are the sources:
