The Bride and Groom

Miriam Rubinstein

Bride

Miriam Rubinstein, daugther of Abigail and Daniel Rubinstein, is a 28 year old hebrew teacher from Alon Shevut, Israel. She moved to Washington D.C. in 2015 when she was 21 years old to escape from the violence in her hometown. While Miriam considers herself a conservative Jew, Miriam's parents are Orthodox so Miriam and Mohammed will be including some orthodox traditions such as gender separation, Yichud, and circling.

Mohammed Hamza

Groom

Mohammed Asim Hamza is a 31 year old Sunni Muslim from Jerusalem, Israel. He moved to Washington D.C. in 2013 to work as a Research Analyst with the department of Justice. Family is very important to Mohammad. After the death of his father, Mohammed Aayan Hamza, Mohammed Asim became the main financial support for his family in Jerusalem. He is very sad that his mother, Sonia Hamza, and his sister, Rida Hamza can't attend the wedding due to financial hardships, but they will be there in spirit.

How they Met

In the Spring of 2017, Miriam Rubinstein and Mohammed Asim Hamza bumped into each other in the kosher meat section of Moti's Market in Rockville, Maryland. After a short chat and a laugh, they proceeded to run into each other 3 more times that day in various sections of the store. After running into Miriam at Moti's a fourth time a week later, Mohammed decided to ask Miriam on a date, and they have been together ever sense.

Over the years, Miriam and Mohammed have bonded over a love of Israeli foods and the stuggles of adjusting to American culture on their own. Even though Miriam and Mohammed care deeply about their respective religions, they have decided to continue practicing separately, so neither has to convert.
